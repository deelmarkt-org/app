## 1. Context (the "why")

`pubspec.yaml:17` declares `intl: any`. The lockfile resolves it to **`0.20.2`** today вЂ” but `any` allows the constraint solver to pick **any future major release**, including breaking ones. In a marketplace where price, distance and date formatting are user-facing legal artifacts (BTW + currency + delivery dates), a silent transitive bump of `intl` could:

- alter the rendered Euro string (`в‚¬ 45,00` в†” `EUR 45,00`) в†’ ACM "verified pricing" exposure under EU Omnibus 2019/2161
- shift `DateFormat` parsing for NL locale в†’ wrong "ship-by" deadlines on transactions
- break golden screenshots silently because formatting glyphs differ

CLAUDE.md В§8 (Quality Gates) requires reproducible builds; `intl: any` defeats this.

**Single direct consumer in production code:** `lib/core/utils/formatters.dart` вЂ” uses `NumberFormat`, `DateFormat`, `initializeDateFormatting`. `easy_localization ^3.0.7` is a transitive consumer (this is the historical reason `any` was used вЂ” to avoid version-conflict friction).
